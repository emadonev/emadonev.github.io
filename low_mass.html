The Sun

The Sun is a star closer to the low mass part of the scale, but still really big compared to some low mass stars. The lower the mass the slower it takes for it to use up the recources (hydrogen,...) so they shine much longer - about a TRILLION YEARS!

The Sun only shines for 12 billion years and currently it is 5 billion years old so it is aproaching middle age.

How will the sun end it's life?

The Sun has since it was born been fusing hydrogen into helium.

Then when it fuzes all of it's hydrogen the core starts to contract and heat up and then the conditions will be right for hydrogen fussion outside of the core.

Then the Sun swell turning into a sub-giant and changing it's color to red.

Then the Sun swelled up to a red giant.

Then the Sun will blow away at least one third of the Suns mass.

Then finally the conditions will be right for helium fussion and then the core itself will begin to grow and absorb most of the energy. Then a recap of everything that just happened will happen, ecept that this time it is helium fusing into carbon.

Finally there is no more hellium to fuze so fuzion stops. By that time the Sun has already blown of it's outer layers and now what's left is a naked core called a white dwarf.

But what about the Earth?

Well the Earth would have already been good and fried even before the Sun started swelling and then the Sun might even eat the Earth. But that might not be the case. As the Sun grew the gravity will decrease and Earth will move into a bigger orbit and avoid feeling what it would be like inside a star...
