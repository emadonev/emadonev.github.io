Gravity

What is gravity?

Gravity is a force that was first discribed by Sir Issac Newton on July 5th 1687. Thanks to a falling apple ...

Newton probably thought: "Why did that apple fall?" and then went to discover why. He came upon the theory of gravity that claims that: Gravity is a force attracting everything to the center of a object it is on.

That is why we can stand on Earth and why objects fall to the ground when you let them go.

Fun fact!

The force of gravity on Earth is 9.807 m/s^2. So how much is ti affecting you? Well I weigh 30 kg so 9.807 * 30 = 294.21 N ( gravity is measured in Newtons - N )! That is the force of gravity affecting me. Give it a try!
